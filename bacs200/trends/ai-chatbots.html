AI chatbots are beginning to replace humans

Make sure a robot doesn't take your job!

Posted by Matthew Davis on December 1, 2021

When you are browsing the web, do you ever come across one of those pop-ups that ask if you need assistance finding something on a website? Chances are, if you interacted with one of those, you were speaking to a robot. Maybe you didn't even realize it!

AI powered chatbots can mimic human conversations, and answer questions almost instantly. Through the use of machine learning these chatbots can be easily mistaken for human.

When did this start?

Recent statistics have shown that 24.9% of consumers used chatbots to interact with businesses, where about 13% did in 2018

This was likely due to the large advances that have recently been made on deep and machine learning, so chatbots are easier for humans to interact with.

Another reason behind this is known as mobile app fatigue. Basically, if you were to develop a really good mobile app, chances are, people would have a hard time finding it due to the overcrowded mobile application market. So, brands instead rely on chatbots through existing messaging channels in order to advertise their products.

In addition to all this, popularity of instant messaging apps is growing. According to 2019 statistics from Statisa, people sent over 41 million mobile messages in one minute! It is no secret that people seem to prefer immediate communications.

What benefiets do they have?

Along with providing the user answers to their questions in seconds to miliseconds, chatbots have proven to be very cost-efficient.

In fact, according to Juniper Research, adopting chatbots for certain organisations would save them $11bn by 2023.

If you werent already afraid of having your job taken by a robot, now would be a good time to start panicking.
